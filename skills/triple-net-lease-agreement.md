# Commercial Triple Net Lease Agreement

Converts transaction facts (term sheets, LOIs, due-diligence packets) into a complete,
execution-ready U.S. commercial NNN lease. Allocates rent, taxes, insurance, and
maintenance to the tenant with full expense pass-through mechanics, use controls, SNDA
provisions, and transfer gates. Includes default/remedy architecture, indemnity/insurance
requirements, guaranty mechanics, environmental allocation, and exhibit templates.

## Quick Start — Collect Before Drafting

1. **Deal terms** — term sheet/LOI, rent schedule, concessions, TI allowance, pre-close conditions, exclusivity/co-tenancy requirements.
2. **Parties** — legal names, entity type, state of formation, good-standing status, signing authority (attach secretary's certificate or operating agreement excerpt), guarantor details, community-property state check for spousal consent.
3. **Property** — legal description, parcel ID, site map, title commitment, recorded encumbrances (CC&Rs, easements, operating covenants), zoning confirmation, occupancy/delivery status.
4. **Environmental** — Phase I/II status, known hazmat, underground storage tanks, historical use disclosures, applicable state environmental statutes.
5. **Risk** — insurance binders/baselines, required coverage levels, lender-required endorsements, indemnity expectations, state anti-indemnity statute check.
6. **Operations** — permitted use, exclusive use/co-tenancy requirements, hours, signage, parking, access needs, ADA responsibility allocation.
7. **Financing** — existing mortgage, lender-required SNDA form, recording requirements for leases over statutory term threshold.
8. **Jurisdiction** — governing-state landlord-tenant statute, anti-indemnity limits, fee-shifting rules, assignment restrictions, enforcement caps, notice service rules, recording thresholds.

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Intake Validation

Confirm data across ten categories before drafting. **Do not draft around missing material inputs — flag and hold.**

`PARTIES | PREMISES | RENT/TERM | EXPENSES | USE/COMPLIANCE | DEFAULTS | MORTGAGE/SNDA | ENVIRONMENTAL | GUARANTY | EXECUTION`

### Step 2 — Draft Modules

| Module | Key Content | Critical Flags |
|---|---|---|
| Definitions | Consistent defined terms, no alternating synonyms | Cross-reference audit required before finalizing |
| Premises & Title | Legal description, use areas, common-area rights, recorded encumbrances | Confirm landlord can grant all rights conveyed |
| Term | Start trigger, possession milestones, rent commencement, renewal | Define "delivery" with specificity — permits, TI completion, certificate of occupancy |
| Base Rent | Rent, due dates, proration, late charges, returned-check fee | Specify payment method; confirm proration formula |
| Escalation | CPI (specify exact index + base month + floor/ceiling), FMV (appraisal mechanism), or fixed step-up | Notice obligation if FMV reset; cap uncapped CPI exposure |
| NNN Expenses | Taxes, insurance, CAM, utilities, reserves | Separate capital items (amortize per useful life) from operating; include management fee cap (typically 3–5%); attach exclusion list; define reconciliation timing and audit rights with response deadline |
| Insurance | CGL, property, excess, WC, umbrella, lender-required endorsements | Additional insured, primary/non-contributory, lapse-cure window, prohibition on self-insurance without landlord consent |
| Maintenance | Structural vs. non-structural split, capital replacement treatment | ADA structural upgrade allocation — assign expressly; do not leave to statutory default |
| Environmental | Pre-existing condition carve-out, hazmat handling obligations, Phase I/II reps, statutory disclosure compliance | Landlord indemnifies pre-existing; tenant indemnifies operations-period contamination |
| Use & Compliance | Permitted/prohibited use, exclusive use, co-tenancy (retail), ADA, OFAC/AML reps | Zoning alignment; change-of-use triggers lender/landlord consent |
| Alterations | Consent standards, ownership at expiry, restoration obligations | Specify which alterations survive vs. must be removed; tie to surrender standard |
| Surrender & Restoration | Physical condition at expiration, fixture/FF&E removal, personal property deemed abandoned after X days | Attach surrender checklist as exhibit; distinguish trade fixtures from leasehold improvements |
| Transfers | Assignment/subletting/change-of-control, consent criteria, recapture rights | Measurable consent standards; continued guarantor liability post-transfer |
| Defaults | Monetary + non-monetary triggers, cure windows, grace period limits | Distinguish first-default cure from repeat-default (no re-cure for same breach within 12 months) |
| Remedies | Reentry, reletting, acceleration, self-help after cure expiry, damages | Jurisdiction-compliant remedy ladder; include self-help with notice and cost recovery |
| Guaranty | Personal/corporate guaranty, burn-down provisions, spousal consent (community property states), notice of default to guarantor | Guaranty must be separately signed; confirm independent consideration |
| Risk Allocation | Indemnity, subrogation waiver, liability split, consequential damage waiver | State anti-indemnity statute check; negligence carve-outs; cap aggregate liability |
| Mortgage Relations | SNDA/attornment — distinguish automatic subordination vs. executed SNDA agreement | Lender-form SNDA approval process; non-disturbance protection for tenant is material |
| Estoppel | Obligation, turnaround deadline (10–15 business days), deemed-approval if no response, liability cap for errors | Do not treat as boilerplate — missed estoppel timing creates lender/buyer closing risk |
| Final Provisions | Notice (addresses, method, receipt rules), force majeure (exclude rent obligations), integration, severability, recording/short-form lease | Confirm state notice service requirements; attach short-form lease for recording if term exceeds state threshold |
| Execution Kit | Signature blocks, authority certificates, exhibit list, closing checklist | All exhibits must be attached at execution — no "to be attached" placeholders |

### Step 3 — Populate Clause Summary

Complete for each lease before finalizing:

- Lease Name / Property Address / Parcel ID
- Parties (legal name, entity type, state, signing authority confirmed)
- Guarantor(s) and guaranty type
- Initial Term / Renewal Terms / Renewal Notice Deadline
- Rent Commencement Trigger (defined delivery standard)
- Base Rent / Escalation Method (index, base period, floor, ceiling)
- Security Deposit / Letter of Credit terms
- NNN Pass-Through Scope / CAM Exclusion List / Management Fee Cap
- Insurance (limits, forms, additional insured, lender endorsements)
- ADA Responsibility Allocation
- Environmental Baseline and Indemnity Split
- Default Cure Periods (monetary / non-monetary / repeat default)
- Self-Help Rights and Notice Requirement
- SNDA Status (executed / automatic subordination / lender form required)
- Estoppel Deadline and Deemed-Approval Mechanic
- Attorney Fees / Dispute Venue / Governing Law
- Notice Addresses and Required Service Method
- Surrender Condition Standard
- Recording Requirement and Short-Form Lease Status

### Step 4 — Compliance Controls (Apply Before Finalizing)

1. **Defined-term audit** — confirm every defined term is used consistently; eliminate synonym drift (e.g., "Premises" must not alternate with "Leased Premises").
2. **Computable variables** — every variable rate (rent, escalation, CAM, taxes) must reference a computable, external, verifiable source.
3. **Anti-indemnity check** — run governing state's anti-indemnity statute; add required carve-outs and caps; tag `[VERIFY]` where outcome is uncertain.
4. **Exhibit completeness** — all exhibits must be attached and complete at execution: Exhibit A (legal description), Exhibit B (rent schedule), Exhibit C (rules & regulations), Exhibit D (form SNDA), Exhibit E (surrender checklist), Exhibit F (guaranty form). Mark unresolved items `[____]`; do not execute with open blanks.
5. **Authority package** — obtain and attach: secretary's certificate or operating agreement excerpt confirming signing authority; entity good-standing certificate; spousal consent if community property state applies to guarantor.
6. **Closing checklist** — verify before execution: insurance binders naming landlord and lender as additional insured, executed SNDA (or lender waiver), security deposit or LC delivered, first-period rent proration calculated, corporate authority confirmed, Phase I/II review complete, short-form lease recorded if required.

## Jurisdiction Checks

| Check | Risk | Action |
|---|---|---|
| Anti-indemnity statute | Many states void indemnity for indemnitee's own negligence | Add carve-outs; cap aggregate; tag `[VERIFY]` |
| Recording threshold | Leases over statutory term (often 3–7 years) must be recorded or void against third parties | Prepare short-form lease for recording |
| Holdover/penalty rent | Punitive holdover multiples may be unenforceable | Set defensible escalation (150–200% of last-month rent) |
| Guaranty enforceability | Varies by authority, form, and notice terms | Confirm form + independent consideration + required notice |
| Community property guaranty | Spousal consent required in AZ, CA, TX, WA, NV, NM, ID, WI | Attach separate spousal consent |
| Notice service validity | State-specific form and receipt rules | Use state-appropriate service matrix |
| ADA structural allocation | Courts split on landlord vs. tenant obligation | Assign expressly in lease; do not rely on statutory default |
| Environmental disclosure | Many states require written disclosure of known hazmat | Confirm compliance; attach disclosure exhibit if required |
| Attorney-fee symmetry | Mutuality often required for enforceability | Mirror or affirmatively justify unilateral recovery |

## Non-Negotiable Drafting Standards

- **Never** assume statutory defaults — localize assignment, cure, acceleration, and indemnity to governing state.
- **Never** leave unresolved blanks unmarked — use `[____]` for missing data, `[VERIFY]` for uncertain law.
- **Never** execute with incomplete exhibits — a missing legal description or rent schedule voids enforceability.
- **Always** preserve full NNN economics: taxes, insurance, CAM, utilities with complete pass-through methodology.
- **Always** separate structural repair from ordinary maintenance with unambiguous, exclusive allocation.
- **Always** include measurable consent standards for transfers — "not unreasonably withheld" requires defined criteria.
- **Always** include SNDA/non-disturbance where financing or foreclosure risk exists.
- **Always** carve out pre-existing environmental conditions from tenant's indemnity obligation.
- **Always** define "delivery" — rent commencement tied to an undefined trigger is unenforceable.
- **Always** attach a separate, signed guaranty — never embed guaranty in the lease body.
