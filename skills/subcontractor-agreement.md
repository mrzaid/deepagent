# Subcontractor Agreement

Drafts enforceable U.S. subcontractor agreements for construction and service projects. Addresses scope, payment, lien rights, insurance, indemnification, schedule, and state-specific compliance. **Do not begin drafting until all prerequisites are cleared.** State law governs nearly every risk-allocation provision — a clause enforceable in Texas may be void in California or New York.

---

## Prerequisites — Gather Before Drafting

**1. Prime Contract** — fully executed, with all exhibits; identify all flow-down obligations (schedule, change order, dispute resolution, insurance, safety, termination, payment); flag any pay-if-paid or no-damage-for-delay clause for state enforceability analysis before drafting; confirm whether owner consent to subcontract is required.

**2. Scope Documents** — plans, specifications, drawings, SOW; shop drawing and submittal requirements; any design/engineering responsibilities assigned to Subcontractor.

**3. Project Details** — state AND county (county controls lien jurisdiction); private vs. public; if public, identify funding source (federal triggers Davis-Bacon, Buy America, Miller Act); start date, completion date, milestones.

**4. Party Information** — full legal names, entity types, states of formation, DBAs; authorized signatories with basis of authority (officer/bylaws; manager/operating agreement); current certificate of good standing for both entities; notice addresses.

**5. License Verification — Hard Stop** — verify Subcontractor's license number, trade classification, and expiration with the project state's licensing board before execution. In California (B&P §7031) an unlicensed subcontractor cannot enforce payment rights; consequences vary by state but are universally significant. Do not execute without confirmed verification.

**6. Insurance & Bonding** — Contractor's required coverage types and limits; additional insureds required under prime contract; whether Miller Act (federal >$150K) or applicable Little Miller Act requires performance/payment bonds at the subcontract tier.

**7. Payment Structure** — lump sum, unit price, T&M, or cost-plus; retainage percentage (verify against state statutory cap before drafting); for T&M/cost-plus, confirm audit rights are included.

## Pre-Drafting Checklist — Clear Every Item Before Writing

**[ ] Public vs. Private Classification** — public/federally funded projects require: wage determination attached as exhibit; Davis-Bacon certified payroll; Buy America flow-down (federal); Miller Act or Little Miller Act bonds. Private projects: confirm no public funding lurks (grants, tax credits, PPP financing can trigger prevailing wage in some states).

**[ ] Preliminary Notice / Lien Rights** — most states require Subcontractor to serve a preliminary notice within 20 days of first furnishing labor or materials or lien rights are permanently lost. This deadline runs from first furnishing, not from execution — calendar it immediately upon confirmed work start. Identify the statutory form, recipients (owner, lender, GC), and service method. On bonded projects, payment bond claim deadlines (often 90 days after last furnishing) are separate and may be shorter than lien deadlines — calendar both.

**[ ] Pay-If-Paid vs. Pay-When-Paid** — these are legally opposite and not interchangeable. Pay-when-paid: Contractor must pay Subcontractor within a reasonable time regardless of owner payment. Pay-if-paid: true condition precedent shifting owner non-payment risk to Subcontractor — requires explicit, conspicuous language and is void in CA, disfavored in NY, and requires precise "condition precedent" wording in TX/FL/GA to be enforceable. Confirm client intent; draft the correct version; never use ambiguous hybrid language.

**[ ] Retainage Cap** — verify project state statutory cap before drafting; a provision exceeding the cap is void as to the excess. Common caps: CA 5%, TX 10% (public: 5% after 50%), FL 10%, NY 5% after 50%, IL 10%. Some states require retainage held in trust — verify.

**[ ] Prompt Payment Statute** — every state mandates payment timelines and a minimum interest rate for late payment. Subcontract payment timing must comply; include the statutory interest rate (or confirm contractual rate is not below the statutory floor).

**[ ] Anti-Indemnity Statute** — determine project state classification before writing any indemnification language:
- **Type I** (CA, TX, FL, CO): void if covers indemnitee's *sole* negligence — shared negligence indemnity permitted
- **Type II** (NY, IL, WA): void if covers *any* negligence of indemnitee — mutual indemnity for respective negligence only
- Draft indemnification after completing this analysis. Never use a form clause without verification.

**[ ] No-Damage-for-Delay** — if flowing down from prime contract, assess enforceability in project state; NY courts and several others restrict or void these provisions for owner-caused delays. If including, always carve out: active interference, fraud, abandonment, and delays of unreasonable duration.

**[ ] Signatory Authority** — confirm actual authority: corporate officer per bylaws; LLC manager/member per operating agreement; general partner per partnership agreement. Title alone does not establish authority.

## Section Outline

| # | Section | Key Contents |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Parties & Recitals | Legal names, license numbers, prime contract reference, project description, no-default representations |
| 2 | Definitions | All capitalized terms; no undefined "reasonable," "promptly," or "completion" |
| 3 | Scope of Work | Included/excluded work, submittals, materials, permits, written change order requirement |
| 4 | Contract Price & Payment | Price structure, invoicing, payment timing, retainage, pay-if-paid or pay-when-paid, disputed amounts, back-charge notice, late payment interest, audit rights (T&M/cost-plus) |
| 5 | Lien Rights & Preliminary Notice | Preliminary notice obligations, statutory lien waiver forms, bond claim rights on bonded projects, no advance waiver |
| 6 | Schedule | Start/completion dates, milestones, delay notice, concurrent delay, liquidated damages, no-damage-for-delay (state-compliant), suspension rights, force majeure |
| 7 | Insurance & Bonding | CGL, workers' comp, auto, professional liability, umbrella; additional insured (primary/non-contributory); waiver of subrogation; certificates before work starts; performance/payment bonds |
| 8 | Indemnification | State-compliant defense/indemnify/hold-harmless; duty to defend; WC exclusivity note; survival |
| 9 | Safety & Hazardous Materials | OSHA compliance, written safety plan, right to stop work for safety violations, Subcontractor responsible for citations, hazardous materials discovery protocol |
| 10 | Warranty | Period, written claim process, response timeline, owner-direct warranty, manufacturer warranty assignment |
| 11 | Intellectual Property | Project documents as work-for-hire; assignment fallback; Subcontractor retains background IP with license to Contractor |
| 12 | Changes | Written change order required; constructive change notice obligation; no oral modifications |
| 13 | Termination | For cause (curable: 5–10 day cure; incurable/safety/insolvency: none); for convenience (work performed + demobilization; no lost profit on unperformed work); suspension >30 days = convenience termination election; Subcontractor's right to stop work and terminate for non-payment (7-day notice); materials/equipment on site at termination |
| 14 | Sub-Subcontractors | Prior written approval required; flow-down of insurance, safety, lien waiver, prevailing wage; Subcontractor remains fully responsible |
| 15 | Compliance | License maintenance with lapse notification; prevailing wage/Davis-Bacon with certified payroll exhibit; Buy America (federal projects); independent contractor status; taxes |
| 16 | Dispute Resolution | Governing law (project state); tiered: negotiation (10 days) → mediation (shared cost) → arbitration or litigation (confirm client preference); fee shifting; jury waiver (where enforceable; confirm intent); work continues pending resolution |
| 17 | General Provisions | Severability, integration, assignment restriction, notices, counterparts, e-signatures |
| 18 | Execution | Signature blocks with authority recitals; entity type-appropriate execution |

## Insurance Minimums

| Coverage | Limits | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Commercial General Liability | $1–2M occurrence / $2–4M aggregate | Contractor, Owner, Architect, Lender as additional insureds — primary, non-contributory |
| Workers' Compensation | Statutory | Employer's Liability $1M/$1M/$1M |
| Commercial Auto | $1M CSL | If any vehicles used on or traveling to/from site |
| Professional Liability | $1M per claim | Required if any design or engineering scope |
| Umbrella / Excess | $5M recommended on larger projects | Must follow form over CGL, auto, employer's liability |

All policies: 30-day cancellation notice (10-day non-payment); waiver of subrogation; certificates and endorsements due before Work commences — do not authorize start without confirmed receipt.

## Post-Execution Checklist

- [ ] Both parties executed; signatory authority documented
- [ ] Certificates of insurance and additional insured endorsements received — no work starts without them
- [ ] Performance and payment bonds received if required
- [ ] Preliminary notice deadline calendared from date of first furnishing (not execution date)
- [ ] Payment bond claim deadline calendared separately (bonded projects)
- [ ] Wage determination attached as exhibit (public/federally funded projects)
- [ ] Statutory lien waiver forms attached as exhibits (mandatory-form states)
- [ ] All notice deadlines calendared: delay notice, warranty response, dispute claim window
- [ ] Project management system updated: Contract Price, retainage %, payment schedule, completion date, insurance expirations
- [ ] Sub-subcontractor approval process active if applicable

## Flags — Escalate Before Proceeding

- Subcontractor not licensed in project state for applicable trade classification — **stop**
- Pay-if-paid desired in CA, NY, or other restrictive state — flag before drafting
- Retainage exceeds project state's statutory cap — void as to excess; restructure
- Public project missing prevailing wage, certified payroll, or Davis-Bacon provisions — debarment exposure
- Federal funding without Buy America flow-down — federal sanctions exposure
- Indemnification not yet analyzed against project state's anti-indemnity statute — do not use placeholder language
- Preliminary notice deadline within 20 days of confirmed work start — calendar and notify client immediately
- Hazardous materials known or suspected on site — include discovery/abatement protocol before work commences
- Prime contract pay-if-paid, no-damage-for-delay, or arbitration clause not yet reviewed by client in context of flow-down
- Design or engineering scope — professional liability insurance and IP provisions require additional attention
- Subcontractor underwent recent merger, restructuring, or name change — verify entity status and authority with extra care
